ICELAND SNOW SKI CABIN
DESIGNED AT ZO THINKS
Icelandic Refuge :

Our cabin, “Echoes of the Elements” embodies the narrative of Iceland’s formation and its stark contrasts, reflecting the dramatic interplay of ice and fire. Inspired by Iceland’s volcanic origin and natural rock formations, the cabin is designed to be more than just a stop for skiers; it aims to serve as the heart hub for the Myvatn area, providing a warm and vibrant sanctuary amidst the harsh landscape. The layout of the cabin is inspired by natural rock formations, creating a central space that serves as a beacon of warmth and gathering. This central space is enveloped by four complementary spaces, each designed to maximize nature connection and views while allowing for optimal wind flow during summer. These separations, inspired by volcanic lateral flanks, act as secondary beacons of warmth, fostering a sense of community

At the heart of our design philosophy lies the essence of Iceland itself—its captivating narrative of elemental contrasts. Just as Iceland emerged from volcanic activity to become the land of fire and ice, our cabin pays homage to this geological tale. From afar, its silhouette stands stark and dramatic against the landscape, mirroring Iceland’s raw, untamed beauty. Yet, upon closer inspection, one discovers a sanctuary alive with warmth and vibrancy, echoing the resilience and spirit of Icelanders. Beyond its role as a ski cabin, our cabin aspires to be the beating heart of the Myvatn region, a hub where residents, tourists, and skiers converge to connect, explore, and immerse themselves in the rich tapestry of Icelandic culture and nature.

The layout of the cabin is inspired by natural rock formations, creating a central space that serves as a beacon of warmth and gathering. This central space is enveloped by four complementary spaces, each designed to maximize nature connection and views while allowing for optimal wind flow during summer. These separations, inspired by volcanic lateral flanks, act as secondary beacons of warmth, fostering a sense of community and connection.

Sensory Narrative: 

The sensory experience of the cabin offers a striking contrast between the outside and the inside. Visitors approach the cabin through the stark, cold landscape, greeted by mirrored surfaces reflecting the sky and surroundings, heightening their sense of exposure. As they get closer, each element (timber, water, sky, mirror, rock, and fire) intertwines into the narrative, creating a journey from the harshness of nature to the cozy warmth of the central space. Inside, the crackling fire pit, warm lighting, wood and stone textures, and the scent of Icelandic snacks and hot cocoa create an inviting atmosphere. The seamless connection to the sky through glass elements enhances this experience, blending comfort with nature’s beauty.

Through the Seasons:

​The cabin’s design seamlessly blends with the Icelandic landscape through all seasons, day and night. In winter, the mirrored glass reflects the icy surroundings, while in summer, it mirrors the lush greenery and vibrant skies. At night, the interior warm lights wash over the basalt walls, transforming the cabin into a beacon of warmth. This play of materials ensures that the cabin remains in harmony with its environment, offering a consistent yet dynamic visual experience throughout the year.

Harnessing the Elements: 

​Our design harnesses natural elements to create a sustainable and self sufficient cabin. Geothermal energy from the site provides heating, hot water, and electricity, utilized through underfloor heating and glass water walls that both heat and enhance aesthetics. Rainwater is collected via sloped masses, channeling it to underground storage for various uses. With no sewage connection, composting toilets are used, converting waste into compost and resonating with nature. This integration of water, heat, and energy management not only meets the cabin’s needs but also contributes to the surrounding environment, making the building an eco-friendly sanctuary.
