THE NEST

The project is based on the idea of nature as a teacher, where the building acts as a natural organism. It is formed of three angular clusters aligned and linked at the rooftop, with a shed above that covers 80% of the building as a horizontal element on the front elevation. The shed creates semi-public spaces beneath, providing protection while maintaining openness and light. The edges act as low walls that guide movement and define outdoor areas, emphasizing clarity in circulation. The facade is simple, white paint with window strips, allowing the shed to remain the unifying element that organizes the clusters into one collective, human-centered community. The building is designed to foster connection, healing, and shared experience while preserving hierarchy and openness.
