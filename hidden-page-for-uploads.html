​WANAS

This project is a cultural center designed for Palestinian refugee children between the ages of 6 and II, where learning, healing. and identity are experienced primarily through the five senses. Many of these children grow up in environments that limit play. exploration, and emotional expression. The center responds by offering a safe place where children can see, hear, smell, touch. and taste their way through culture, play, and self-discovery.


The building is organized as a circular form with an open playground at its center. This central space acts as the heart of sensory interaction, where movement, sound, and social play naturally mix. Around this core, a series of workshops are arranged, each focusing on one or more senses. Gardening and planting allow children to smell and care for living things. Cooking activities connect taste with memory and tradition.

Material workshops such as fabric and wood invite touch The workshops are connected by an elevated system of bridges that crosses the building and is visible from the outside. As children move along the bridges, they can see, hear, or smell the next activity before arriving, turning transition into anticipation. The bridges change in scale and shape, sometimes widening into small platforms or narrowing into passages, and include places to sit, rest, and listen. In this way, movement becomes a sensory experience itself.

The bridges are enclosed and protective, offering safety while allowing filtered sensory connections. The architecture carefully balances openness and enclosure, activity and rest. By organizing space around the five senses, the center helps children build curiosity, explore themselves, reconnect with their culture, and form lasting emotional and sensory memories through play.

BY: HANA HOSSAM
