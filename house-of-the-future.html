HOUSE OF THE FUTURE
DESIGNED AT TWO EMMS


NAWAH
“A home within a home.”
​

​A simple statement yet defining a strong conceptual base on which NAWAH is designed. This house is treated as an ecosystem that adapts, changes and expands to make the users feel that it is a home, not just a house. Each module of this house is perceived as a living ecosystem that could be self-sustained, sustainable and flexible, according to the user’s needs. Not only that, it could also be mutated and adaptive to the social as well as environmental changes, not only within the family, but throughout the city as a whole.

The house pays tribute to the strong heritage of the city, and the traditions of the inhabitants. It is designed to allow privacy, and simultaneously gatherings, and also serenity but not exclusion from context, surroundings and heritage. It allows the users to build a sense of connection to each part of the different modules forming the whole house.

​This residence replicates the idea of a living organism, acting as the main nucleus around which this ecosystem is formed. Having a main spine of water inside the house metaphors into the beating heart of the space, that give life to each one of its units, which co-exist together to generate this ecosystem.

This house also plays on the idea of challenging a modern approach of design, whether in materials or architecture, but at the same time, it is just a new adaptation of the typical traditional Emirati house, utilizing vernacular environmental techniques, as well as considering the social culture in the user experience inside the house.

Nawah is designed greatly with environmental aspects in mind, to achieve substantiality that is well integrated with architecture. Examples are shown in the building mass itself, which is translated into a linear layout, creating high air pressure points between the masses and the house fins, for better air circulation. This also happens in the main wind corridor of the house, acting as the vertical+ horizontal spine, that distributes the air. The interplay between the indoor and outdoor zones helps in filtering and cooling the air too. Another feature added to the house, are the fins, which are designed to minimize the solar radiation on the surface walls.

The aesthetic aspects were also considered while designing the house. Using the fins wasn’t just for environmental purposes, but also architecturally, it’s a metaphor of the idea of layering, reflecting the value of privacy that any Arab family is keen to protect. The play of the masses gives this mystery feeling of the interior protection of space, and camouflages in the context, physically and metamorphically.

This collage is an interpretation of the conceptual idea of layering. The UAE is a country of a very rich heritage, yet it is very open to modernization, without losing the seeds of its culture. NAWAH is designed to bring this juxtaposition of ideas and manifest it in how the individuals are living nowadays. It plays subtly on the harmony between private and public, modern and ethnic, desert and sea, and finally heritage and future.

Form generation

The house is designed to be adjustable and changeable according to the users’ needs. It expands and shrinks by using movable partitions made from burlap ( jute fabric ), and glass walls. Each space can be interchanged according to the function needed, and transformed into either an indoor or an outdoor space. This again plays on the idea of layers and camouflage, in terms of deciding when the house is open to its context, or inverted into the interior courtyards.
