Teaching As part of the design process.

Design 4 Studio

Teaching is part of the design process. I mentor because I believe design thinking is not something you absorb passively — it has to be provoked, challenged, and practiced. The same curiosity I bring to every project is what I bring into the studio with students.

My approach to teaching mirrors my approach to design: begin with questions, not answers. I push students to dig into the why behind a brief before touching a tool. From there, we work through extraction, exploration, and iteration together — developing not just the project, but the designer's ability to think independently and critically.

I currently work as a Teaching Assistant in the Architecture Department at AASTMT, where I support design studios and visualization courses. Whether it's guiding a complex spatial brief or helping a student communicate their idea with precision and clarity, the goal is always the same: help them find their own design voice.

This section documents the courses, briefs, and student work I've been part of. It's not just a record — it's a reflection of an ongoing conversation between design education and practice.
